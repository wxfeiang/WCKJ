Lu Gaoqing —— HEAD OF LABORATORY

The world-renowned expert in material chemistry and the leader of Australian nanotechnology have profound knowledge in the preparation of nanomaterials and their application in clean energy sources. Professor Kui Gaoqing is now Vice President of Queensland University in Australia. He has been in charge of research exchanges (since 2008) and vice president of scientific research (since 2009), as well as executive vice president (2012), who is also the first Vice President of the University of Australia in the history of China.

High definition is devoted to the research of new generation functional nanomaterials. Through manual assembly and molecular assembly, nanoparticles, nanotubes, nanowires, nano-thin films, nanoporous structures and nanocomposites are prepared. New generation functional nanomaterials are developed, such as new silica-titanium dioxide composite photocatalysts, hydrogen separation and energy storage materials, and nanoparticles. Drug carrier; A new type of titanium dioxide composite photocatalyst with silica gel as carrier was invented, such as new silica-titanium dioxide composite photocatalysts.

ACHIEVEMENTS IN SCIENTIFIC RESEARCH

lu gaoqing is a well-known scientist in the fields of molecular sieves, porous materials, carbon materials, nanomaterials and technologies, and adsorption and catalysis. He has done many pioneering work in this field, such as narrowing and repairing the channels into MCM-41 molecular sieves through layers of superimposed molecules; his review articles on C-NOx reaction and MCM-41 materials are standard references; and his research on pillared clays is also pioneering and remarkable. In the field of mesoporous materials, carbon materials and pillared clay materials, some significant work has been done, such as the synthesis of nanocrystalline alumina by template method, the modification and characterization of mesoporous MCM41 molecular sieve .
