CYTOPATHIC EFFECT (CPE) REFERS TO THE DENATURATION OF CELLS CAUSED BY VIRUS INFECTION ON TISSUE CULTURED CELLS, WHICH IS A SIGN OF INFECTION. CPE CAN BE OBSERVED BY PHASE CONTRAST MICROSCOPY OR FLUORESCENCE MICROSCOPY, BUT IT CAN PRODUCE PHOTOTOXICITY. IN THIS STUDY, WE USED NANOLIVE DIGITAL HOLOGRAPHIC TOMOGRAPHY (DHTM) TO REVEAL THE MODE OF VIRUS INFECTION IN LIVING CELLS IN A UNIQUE AND MINIMAL INTERFERENCE WAY. DHTM RECORDS THE PHASE SHIFTS OF LOW-DOSE LIGHT THROUGH TRANSPARENT SAMPLE SURFACES IN AN UNMARKED MANNER. DHTM INFERS 3-D (3D) TOMOGRAPHIC IMAGES BASED ON REFRACTIVE INDEX (RI). BY MEASURING RI AND CALCULATING THE REFRACTIVE INDEX GRADIENT (RIG), DHTM REVEALS THE HETEROGENEITY OF CELLS IN VIRAL INFECTION OPTICALLY. WE FOUND THAT RIG INCREASED GRADUALLY IN VACCINIA VIRUS (VACV), HERPES SIMPLEX VIRUS (HSV) AND RHINOVIRUS (RV) INFECTION. VACV, RATHER THAN HSV AND RV INFECTION, CAUSES CHANGES IN CELL VOLUME, BUT ALL THREE VIRUSES ALTER THE DYNAMICS OF PLASMA MEMBRANE AND INDUCE APOPTOSIS, SIMILAR TO CHEMICAL COMPLEX ONCOGENE INHIBITORS, WITH VIRUS-SPECIFIC CHARACTERISTICS.
