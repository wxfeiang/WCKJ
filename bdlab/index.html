BELL LABORATORY

Laboratory Introduction

Bell Laboratories, formerly known as Bell Telephone Laboratories, was founded in 1925 and headquartered in the United States (later moved to Melbourne, New Jersey). It is a research and development institution with high honor in the world. Its main purpose is to carry out communication science research. There are 20,000 researchers and 6 research departments under it. It has 14 branches and 56 laboratories, with an annual outlay of US$2.2 billion, of which 10% is used for basic research. In addition to radio electronics, it has a high level in solid state physics (including magnetics, semiconductors, surface physics), astrophysics, quantum physics and nuclear physics. Since its establishment, Bell Laboratory has obtained more than 26000 patents (even daily). Among them, more than 50 major scientific achievements, such as sound film, quality control, information theory, laser theory, 3K cosmic background radiation, videophone, magnetic bubble device, optical communication, digital computer, etc., have had an important impact on our life.
